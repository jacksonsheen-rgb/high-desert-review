The masterpiece of any modern American political pundit, is their ability to ebb and flow between claims about the nature of humanity, the inner-workings of the minds of public officials, and their own opinions regarding the recent reconciliation bill, without much regard for evidence in any camp. You can even get a sample for the diet version of this phenomenon by asking anyone on the street why the losing side did not gather the votes to continue holding power. Clearly, the party should have just held the exact opinion of the person you ask. Matt Yglesias has coined this as the "[pundit's fallacy](https://80000hours.org/podcast/episodes/matthew-yglesias-pundits-fallacy-military-intervention/#:~:text=One%20other%20thing%20to%20check,Wiblin%20and%20Matthew%20also%20cover:. )."
